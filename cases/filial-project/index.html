Brand Story

Background

Inspired by a film about entrepreneurship and founded by a Parsons student and NYU student, Filial Project emerged from a desire to blend cultural heritage with contemporary fashion.

Concept

The name "Filial" reflects the Thai value of filial piety—love and gratitude for family. The brand seamlessly blends cultural roots with modern design, creating fashion that is timeless, personal, and inclusive.

Style & Vision

  • Aesthetic: "New Vintage" — nostalgic yet contemporary
  • Materials: Reused and sustainable fabrics
  • Philosophy: Fashion for everyone
  • Origin: Produced in Thailand with textured sleeve details
  • Detail: Decorated with vintage buttons handpicked by NYU co-founder
02

Campaign One

"Stay exactly the way you are."

REAL POSTURE ONLY

You don't need to turn yourself into a lookbook to deserve the clothes. The messiest, loosest, most un-posed version of you is exactly who this piece is made for.

What We Want People to Feel

  • Anyone can wear it
  • Your body doesn't need to be "fixed" or edited to be allowed in the picture
  • This brand is on the side of ordinary, tired, introverted, socially anxious people

Shot Examples: Your Most Real Postures

  • Curled up on the couch scrolling on your phone
  • Sitting on the floor eating takeout
  • Sleeping crooked on the bus or subway
  • Slumped over a desk rushing a deadline
  • Barefoot, sitting cross-legged on a chair

We Emphasize

  • You don't have to show your face
  • You don't have to hold in your stomach
  • You don't have to clean your room first
03

Campaign Two

"I know you're watching, so I'll give you this punch to look at."

BUTTON WATCHING, BUTTON PUNCHING

New York is full of little eyes watching you—cameras, strangers' stares, stereotypes—but you get to punch back in a playful way, and the silver buttons become the perfect excuse to do it on camera.

Overall Mood

  • DV cam texture, street-level, light grain
  • Playful, not afraid of the lens, slightly cheeky and provocative
  • Not angry protest, more like: "I know you're watching, so I'll give you this punch to look at."

The Silver Button Plays Two Roles

  • Little eye: Stuck onto CCTV poles, utility boxes, walls, acting like tiny watching eyes
  • Punch target: When someone throws a punch, the button sits right in front of the fist like a target, but the fist stops just short of hitting anything, hanging in mid-air
